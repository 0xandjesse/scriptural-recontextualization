## Study Recommendations

### Starting Point
**Begin with The Gospel of Thomas** - This is arguably the most important text in Codex II, containing the earliest non-canonical Jesus sayings. It provides a completely different perspective on Jesus from the canonical gospels.

### Priority Texts
1. **Gospel of Thomas** - Foundational for understanding alternate Jesus tradition
2. **Apocryphon of John** - Compare variations with Codex I version
3. **Gospel of Philip** - Deep theological insights on sacraments and knowledge

### Cross-Reference Opportunities
- Compare Apocryphon of John across Codices I, II, III, and IV
- Connect Gospel of Thomas sayings with themes from Gospel of Philip
- Note creation mythology continuity between Hypostasis and Origin texts
- Compare apostolic perspectives (Peter vs. Thomas vs. Philip vs. James)

### Text Classification by Theme
- **Cosmological Texts**: Apocryphon of John, Origin of the World
- **Jesus Tradition**: Gospel of Thomas
- **Sophiological Works**: Gospel of Philip  
- **Psychological Texts**: Exegesis on the Soul
- **Creation/Anthropological**: Hypostasis of the Archons
- **Apostolic Dispute**: Book of Thomas the Contender
